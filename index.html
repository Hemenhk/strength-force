Different Types Of Exercises

The Difference Between Compound and Isolated Exercises.

Compound

Compound exercises are exercises that activate multiple muscle groups at the same time. For example, a deadlift is an exercise that target not only the back, but also the quads, hamstrings, rear delts, biceps and traps, as well as your core. These are exercises that generally require more attention to technique, and as such, we have linked videos that go into further detail as to how to perform them properly, as it is easier to hurt yourself doing them with poor technique.

Isolated

Isolated exercises are exercises that target only one muscle group at one time. For example a bicep curl only target the bicep, unless done wit incredibly poor technique. These exercises are great at sculpting the particular muscle groups, and are a great way to ensure lagging muscle groups are targeted to achieve an aesthetic physique. As an example, calves are a muscle group that men typically neglect at the gym, which causes an imbalanced physique. This can be rectified by isolating the calves with particular exercises.
